Channe Puri Recipe

A delicious and satisfying North Indian dish.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 30 minutes
  • Cook: 45 minutes

Ingredients

For the Channe (Chickpea Curry):

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Water as needed

For the Puri:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

Making the Channe:

  1. Pressure cook the soaked chickpeas with 4 cups of water for 3-4 whistles. Let the pressure release naturally.
  2. Heat oil in a pressure cooker or a deep-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Add the cooked chickpeas and enough water to make a gravy of your desired consistency.
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the flavors are well-combined.
  8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Making the Puri:

  1. In a large bowl, combine whole wheat flour, salt, and oil. Mix well.
  2. Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin, round circle.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Carefully drop the rolled puri into the hot oil and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Remove the puris from the oil and drain on paper towels.
