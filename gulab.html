Gulab Jamun Recipe

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made from deep-fried dough balls soaked in sugar syrup, flavored with cardamom and rose water.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cooking: 30 minutes

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1-2 tbsp ghee
  • 1/4 cup milk (or as needed to form dough)

For the Sugar Syrup

  • 1 and 1/2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional)
  • Few strands of saffron (optional)

For Frying

  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Boil for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Add cardamom powder, rose water, and saffron, then keep warm.
  2. Prepare the Dough: In a bowl, mix milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add ghee and mix until crumbly. Slowly add milk to form a soft, smooth dough. Cover and let rest for 10 minutes.
  3. Shape the Dough: Divide the dough into small, smooth balls without cracks.
  4. Fry the Dough Balls: Heat oil or ghee over low-medium heat. Gently drop the dough balls and fry until golden brown, stirring gently to ensure even cooking. Remove and drain excess oil.
  5. Soak in Syrup: Add the fried balls to the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the syrup fully.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped pistachios or almonds if desired.
