Golgappe (Pani Puri) Recipe

Golgappe, also known as Pani Puri, is a tangy, spicy, and refreshing Indian snack loved by people of all ages.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 30 minutes
  • Cooking: 10 minutes

Ingredients

Puri

  • 1/2 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt, a pinch
  • Oil, for deep frying

Filling

  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • Salt, to taste

Spicy Mint Water

  • 1 cup mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1/2 inch ginger
  • 1 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 4 cups cold water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Puri: In a bowl, mix semolina, all-purpose flour, and a pinch of salt. Add water little by little to make a firm dough. Cover and let rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Roll the dough thinly and cut into small circles. Heat oil in a deep pan, and fry the puris until they puff up and turn golden. Drain and let cool.
  3. Prepare the Filling: Mix the boiled potatoes and chickpeas. Add chaat masala and salt, and mix well. Set aside.
  4. Prepare the Spicy Mint Water: Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, ginger, tamarind pulp, cumin powder, and salt with a little water. Strain the mixture and add cold water to adjust consistency. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Assemble: Gently crack the top of each puri, add a small amount of filling, and pour in a spoonful of spicy mint water.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of flavors!
